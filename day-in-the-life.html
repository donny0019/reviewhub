A Day in the Life of an App Reviewer

By Alex Carter • July 26, 2025

Ever wondered what it's actually like to be an app reviewer? Is it just playing with apps all day? The reality is a mix of focused testing, thoughtful analysis, and clear communication. Hereâ€™s a glimpse into a typical day.

Morning: The Hunt for Opportunities

My day starts like many others: with a cup of coffee and a check of my emails and dashboards. I'm looking for new testing invitations from platforms like ReviewHub. The early bird gets the worm, as popular tests can fill up quickly. I scan the requirements: "Needs Android user in Canada," "Requires experience with project management tools." I find a 20-minute test for a new photo-editing app that pays $15. Perfect. I accept it and schedule it for later in the day.

Afternoon: Deep Dive and Focused Testing

After lunch, it's time to test. I find a quiet room to avoid background noise, as many tests require me to speak my thoughts aloud while recording my screen. I read the instructions carefully. The developer wants me to test the new "AI background removal" feature.

I start the screen recording and begin the test. I narrate my every move: "Okay, I'm opening the app now. The home screen is clean. I'm looking for the new feature... I see a button labeled 'Magic Cut,' I'll tap that." I upload a photo and try the feature. It works, but it's a bit slow. I make a note of the processing time. I try it with a few more photos of varying complexity. On one photo with a lot of fine detail, the app crashes. Bingo. That's a critical bug. I carefully document the steps to reproduce the crash.

Late Afternoon: The Write-Up

With the testing complete, it's time for the final, and most important, step: the written summary. I don't just say "it crashed." I write a detailed report:

Summary: The AI background removal is a promising feature but has performance issues and a critical bug.
Positives: The user interface for accessing the feature is clear and easy to find.
Issues: The processing time for a simple photo was over 15 seconds. The app crashed when trying to process a complex image (see attached photo).
Suggestion: Add a progress bar during processing. Investigate the crash related to high-detail images.

I submit my video recording and written report. Within a few hours, I get a notification: my review has been approved and $15 has been added to my account. It's a satisfying feeling, knowing my feedback will help make an app better, all while earning a little extra from the comfort of my home.
