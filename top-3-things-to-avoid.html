Top 3 Things to Avoid When Reviewing Apps for Money

By Alex Carter • July 24, 2025

Earning money by reviewing apps is a fantastic side hustle, but it requires professionalism. Certain mistakes can get your feedback ignored or even get you removed from testing platforms. Here are the top three things to avoid to ensure you remain a trusted and in-demand reviewer.

1. Avoid Generic and Uninspired Feedback

The most common mistake is providing feedback that could apply to any app. Developers are paying for your unique perspective, not a generic comment. Low-effort reviews are easy to spot and offer zero value.

Example of what to avoid:

"The app is good. It works fine. I liked the colors."

This tells the developer nothing. Why is it good? What works well? Which colors did you like and why? Always strive for depth and specificity in your comments.

2. Avoid Being Dishonest or Overly Positive

It can be tempting to only say nice things, especially if you want more work. However, developers are not paying for praise; they are paying for honesty. A review that only points out positives is just as useless as one that only points out negatives. They need to know what isn't working so they can fix it.

Never invent issues, but also never ignore them. If you're asked to complete a task and it's impossible due to a bug, report it clearly. Your credibility is your most important asset, and dishonesty will quickly ruin it.

3. Avoid Making It All About You

While your personal experience is the basis of the review, remember that you are testing for a broader audience. Don't let personal pet peeves or niche preferences dominate your feedback. A feature you dislike might be essential for the app's core users.

Instead of: "I hate that this photo app has social sharing features. I never share my photos."

Try this: "For users who value privacy, it might be beneficial to make the social sharing features less prominent or offer a 'private mode' during onboarding."

This simple shift in perspective shows that you're thinking about the product's success, not just your own tastes.
