How to Write an App Review That Developers Will Love

By Alex Carter • July 20, 2025

When you're reviewing apps for money, it's easy to think of it as just a task. But to be a top-tier reviewer, you need to provide feedback that developers find genuinely useful. A great review doesn't just point out flaws; it provides actionable insights. Hereâ€™s how to write a review that developers will value and that will get you more high-paying opportunities.

1. Be Specific and Provide Context

Vague feedback is a developer's nightmare. Instead of saying "The app is confusing," describe exactly what confused you. Provide context for your actions.

  • Bad: "I couldn't find the settings menu."
  • Good: "I was trying to change my notification preferences. I first looked for a gear icon on the main dashboard, but couldn't find one. I eventually found the settings under my profile tab, which wasn't intuitive to me."

This level of detail helps developers pinpoint the exact user experience (UX) issue and consider a more logical placement for the feature.

2. Offer Constructive, Balanced Criticism

Your goal is to help improve the app, not to tear it down. Balance your criticism with positive feedback. This shows you're engaged and fair. When you do find an issue, try to suggest a potential solution.

  • Bad: "The checkout button is ugly and in a stupid place."
  • Good: "I really like the clean design of the product pages. However, the checkout button's color doesn't stand out from the background, making it hard to see. Moving it to the bottom of the screen and using a contrasting color like orange might make it more prominent."

3. Report Bugs with Precision

If you encounter a bug, your report is only as good as the information you provide. A simple "The app crashed" is not helpful. To write a useful bug report, include:

  • Steps to Reproduce: List the exact sequence of actions you took that led to the bug.
  • Expected vs. Actual Results: What did you expect to happen? What happened instead?
  • Device and OS Information: Mention your device model (e.g., iPhone 15 Pro) and operating system version (e.g., iOS 18).

4. Focus on the Target Audience

Keep the app's intended audience in mind. A feature that seems unnecessary to you might be critical for the target user. Frame your feedback from the perspective of someone who would use this app every day. For example, when reviewing a complex project management app, your feedback should be more detailed and technical than for a simple mobile game.

By following these guidelines, you'll elevate the quality of your reviews from simple opinions to valuable, professional feedback. This not only helps developers create better products but also establishes you as a reliable and sought-after reviewer on platforms like ReviewHub.
